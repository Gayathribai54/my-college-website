Computer Science

Computer science is the study of computation, information, and automation, focusing on the theory, design, and application of computer systems to solve problems, encompassing everything from mathematical algorithms and software development to artificial intelligence and hardware interactions. It's about understanding how computers work and using that knowledge creatively and logically to build new technologies and efficient solutions, involving programming, data structures, and designing systems that shape our digital world.

A Mathematics Department, often found in universities/institutes, is an academic unit focused on teaching and researching math, covering branches like Algebra, Geometry, Calculus, Statistics, and Applied Math, training students for careers in Data Science, Finance, Engineering, and more by developing problem-solving and logical skills. They foster both pure (abstract) and applied (real-world) mathematics, preparing graduates for diverse fields that rely on quantitative reasoning.

BCOM

A B.Com (Bachelor of Commerce) department focuses on undergraduate studies in business, accounting, and finance, offering core subjects like Financial Accounting, Economics, Business Law, and Taxation, with specializations in areas such as Accounting & Finance, Marketing, or Banking. It's a 3-year program designed to build foundational knowledge for careers in finance, auditing, management, and other commerce-related fields, with options for General or Honours degrees.
